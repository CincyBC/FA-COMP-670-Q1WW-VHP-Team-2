COMP511 - Foundation Data Struc & Obj Orntd Design
Course Title
Foundation Data Struc & Obj Orntd Design
Credit Hours
4
Course Description
This course continues the object-oriented approach to intermediate-level software development. The student will learn and reflect on fundamental object-oriented analysis techniques, basic design patterns, and linear data structures such as lists and queues.
Prerequisites
Complete at least 1 of the following: 
COMP501 - Foundations of Programming (4)
COMP111 - Introduction to Computer Science & Object-Oriented Programming (4)
ITEC136 - Principles of Programming (4)
Outcomes
Analyze real-world problems with object-oriented principles of inheritance and polymorphism.
Design, implement, and test solutions based on object-oriented principles of inheritance and polymorphism.
Detect and correct runtime program errors using exceptions.
Read and write random access and sequential files of text and binary data.
Analyze, code, and execute linear data structures.
Analyze, compare, contrast, and apply basic design patterns.
