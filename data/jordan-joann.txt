Profile
Dr. JoAnn Jordan has over 35 years of professional healthcare experience in a variety of organizational settings and has built a reputation as an energetic and supportive team leader. She is a known subject matter expert in a full spectrum of healthcare domains including public health, healthcare management, health information management, and health informatics, and has used her expertise to develop curricula for educational institutions all across the globe. With her energetic and engaging style, Dr. Jordan is a dynamic contributor to organizational training and professional health resources. Her primary interests are in improving quality health outcomes, employee satisfaction, and efficiency through interdepartmental collaboration and teamwork. Dr. Jordan obtained her BA and MPH from the University of Massachusetts at Amherst and her PhD in Biomedical Informatics from Rutgers University. She is the current Chair of the Allied Healthcare Department and Program Chair for the MS in Health Informatics and BS in Public Health Program. Dr. Jordan is also Lead Faculty for three doctoral Health Informatics courses. As a practitioner of lifelong learning, Dr. Jordan holds multiple credentials including the Certified Health Education Specialist (CHES), Certified Professional in Healthcare Quality (CPHQ), Certified Professional in Health Informatics (CPHI), and Registered Health Information Administrator (RHIA).
